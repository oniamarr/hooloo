Our Manifesto

01

Segregation didn't end when the laws changed. It moved into lunchrooms, into friend groups, into the quiet corners of every school in this country.

Hooloo was born from a personal truth: that young people are watching the world divide itself by color, background, and zip code — and deciding they won't be next. This is not an organization. It's a movement of individuals who choose, every single day, to sit with someone different. To speak first. To refuse the comfortable silence of separation.

We don't wait for policy. We act in the hallways, at the lunch table, on the bus. We are the change that doesn't ask permission.

The Reality

WHY NOW

001

Segregated Schools

Despite decades of legal change, racial and economic segregation in American schools has quietly intensified. The classroom is often the first place a child learns who they're "supposed" to sit with.

002

Social Siloing

Social groups in schools and communities routinely form along racial lines. This isn't always hatred — sometimes it's just comfort, habit, and the absence of a reason to cross over.

003

The Cost of Silence

When young people don't learn to exist across difference, they carry that division into adulthood. The lunch table predicts the boardroom. The school hallway shapes the neighborhood.

How We Move

THE HOOLOO
WAY

🪑

Sit Different

Every week, hooloo members make a conscious choice to sit next to someone outside their usual group. One seat. One conversation. That's where it starts.

SIT
🗣️

Speak First

We train our members to initiate — not wait to be approached. Belonging starts with one person willing to say hello across the invisible line.

SAY
📣

Call It Out

Hooloo members are empowered to name segregation when they see it — not with anger, but with clarity. Naming it is the first act of dismantling it.

NAME
🤝

Build Bridges

We create structured moments — study groups, events, shared projects — designed specifically to mix people across racial and social divides.

BUILD
